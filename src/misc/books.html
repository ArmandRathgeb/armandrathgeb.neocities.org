Non-fiction

Probability Theory: The Logic of Science - E.T. Jaynes Probably the best introduction to probability theory that I've come across. Jaynes was a physicist and the first to draw a parallel between logic and probability theory. The first two chapters focus on how to derive the product and sum rules, from which the rest of probability theory follows by starting with boolean logic and relaxing its constraints. The rest of the book builds the rest of probability theory from the ground up and teaches applicaitons as well. Unfortunately, Jaynes died before completing this magnum opus, but the parts that are completed are fantastic.
I think that it provides a totally different way of thinking about probability. Probability is another way of reasoning under undertainty, hence why it is the logic of science, the ultimate application of making inferences with uncertain information.
I highly recommend reading this book if you work in any sort of scientific or statistical field.
